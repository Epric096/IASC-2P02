For this assignment, I am visualizing the novel The Little Prince written by Antonie de Saint-Exupéry. This is a fictional novel targeting the age group of children to adults. The story is from a pilot's perspective about how he meets the main character, the little prince, in the Sahara desert after an airplane accident. The little prince is coming from another planet, Asteroid 325, and decides to travel around the world because he feels betrayed by a rose on the planet. Little Prince has seen many grown-up worlds, learned many meaningful experiences, and eventually made a deal with the snake to head back to his planet. The novel has a wide imagination and can help build the visualization for this assignment, and each character has a strong connection with the main character.

For the first visualization, I used an octahedron corresponding to the star (planet) representing the characters, because each planet in the novel has a different characteristic person living on them. In the visualization, we can see the little prince appear throughout the story and has a greater density than in the beginning, often colliding with other terms showing the interaction in between. By turning off the prince term we can see the term rose shown in the graph appears a few times in the beginning, significantly increased at the end. Although Rose is a very important character in the novel, but gives little information at the start and leaves an awkward gap in between the graph, this is one insight. The fox is a friend and a teacher of the little prince who appears around the end, interacting with another important character Rose in the visualization. Even though Rose and Fox are both shown together in the visualization, they never meet in the novel, this is another insight. I created a star-drop animation for this visualization to show how the main character connects everything.
